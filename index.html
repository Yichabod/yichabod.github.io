About / Resume / Writing / Projects

I think and write about technology and society. Sometimes I build things too. Lately I’ve been obsessed with the following question:

“How should we govern our digital institutions for humanity’s future to flourish?”

This question naturally spans topics ranging from the factors of technological progress to antitrust law, but right now I’m tackling it by looking at the governance of open source machine learning projects.

I’m a CS Master’s student at MIT, where I also graduated graduated in 2021 with a degree in Computer Science (with much dabbling also in economics and philosophy). Because of a generous grant from the Open Philanthropy project?, I’m directing my own research project and working loosely with a number of ethnographers, developers, and otherwise interdisciplinary people.

In the past, I’ve been a data research analyst at the wonderful (Center for Security and Emerging Technology)[https://cset.georgetown.edu/], a policy intern at the (Office of Science and Technology Policy)[https://www.whitehouse.gov/ostp/], and a researcher at the MIT Computational Cognitive Science group.

Intellectual influences of mine include Elinor Ostrom, Nadia Eghbal, and David Chapman. I am deeply grateful to them and their mutuals for inspiring me and shaping my worldview.

My mailing list is for the occasional personal update and reflection. My substack is for more regular updates on my open source governance research. You can also find me on (Twitter)[https://twitter.com/mslkmp].
