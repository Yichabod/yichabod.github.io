On the peculiarities of Japanese culture:

Gekokujo or “the low bringing down the high“ was a cultural norm, whereby it was acceptable to have planned violence, even in the form of mass murders, for the greater glory of the nation. How was Japan unified in the first place? Wasn’t it feudal till very recently?

The May 15 incident is a good example whereby Prime Minister Inukai, a 75 your old man, who opposed the conquest of Manchuria, was killed by dozens of assassins. Nine young men asked to take the place of those on trial when they had nothing to do with the assassination, and to show the good faith and closed the nine little fingers pickled in a jar of alcohol. Such was the fanaticism of patriotic fervor.

It was a big factor leading to militarism and ultimately self-destructive nationalism; the testosterone-filled young men were the ones who committed gekokujo

The importance of honor. Forty seven ronin is a story enshrined in the Japanese mythos. “In the seventeenth century a provincial lord was so disgraced by Kira, the chief minister of the shogun, that he committed suicice. Oishi, a samurai warrior serving the dishonored man, vowed to avenge his death, and for the next seven years, he pretended, in the tradition of samurai sacrifice, to be a dissolute drunk while secretly planning revenge. Early one morning in a snowstorm, the forty-seven ronin (samurai who had lost their master…) raided the Kira home, not far from the Imperial Palace. They assassinated the chief minister, cut off his head and brought it to the temple where their master’s ashes were enshrined. Then, in true bushido style, all forty-seven committed hara-kiri.

Japan seems to have been a much more hated colonizer than the West. In the Phillipines, the Americans were initially hated but after the years under Japanese rule were so enamored with the Americans that it was said a local leader pledged to work 365 days a year for free for the Americans. Very strong spirit of goodwill, especially for MacArthur

The state of Japan prior to WW2

Japan participated in international treaties and was a functioning member of the League of Nations. However, two key points of tension between Japan and the west was racial equality and Japanese expansion.

The Japanese often felt diminished and seen as lesser because of their race, and for good reason. At the 1919 Paris Peace conference, the Japanese were the only non-western of the five great powers. It called for a ‘racial equality’ clause in the Versailles Peace Treaty but that was struck down by the other nations. Of course, the Japanese themselves were

The second point of tension was around Japanese colonialism. Prior to Commodore Perry’s gunboat diplomacy, the Japanese had pursued a firmly isolationist policy, refusing to trade much with the outer world. After seeing the raw power of the U.S. gunboats, the Meiji restoration (1868) was the beginning of an intense period of trying to mimic the west. They sought to create a country that could withstand becoming a colony themselves. “Rich country, strong military” was such a slogan. You can see the mimicry with the Japanese colonial policy. The recipe was take over local governments, start extracting natural resources to fuel expansion elsewhere.

Taiwan was taken in 1895 after the Japanese victory in the Sino-Japanese war. Korea became a protected by the Japanese after the Russo-Japanese war at the turn of the 20th century.

It’s not entirely clear who, but surely either the Japanese expansionists looking to provoke Japanese invasion of China, or else, Chinese communists, hoping to start a full scale war between Chiang Kai Shek, and the Japanese.  But a third-party attempted to incite skirmishes between the local Chinese and Japanese, who are on good terms. It ended up serving as justification for the expansionists. 

Communications were so bad that Tokyo had no idea the skirmish was being solved and demanded China recognize the local government

Ultimately reinforcements on trigger edge started shooting at each each other. Justification was protecting Japanese lives in Manchuria. They didn’t see it as declaring war with China but rather pushing for negotiation terms with Chiang. Officially they declared aims of achieving a new order and containing communism. Further, in the war, prince konoye , the Prime Minister, feared that uniting Asia was Japan’s last Hope for survival in the face of the Russo German pact.
